The Joyce Poems: #1

by Roger Donway

I might have guessed
at the very start
that it's rash, at best,
for the human heart
to covet nymphs.

I might have seen
in the first glimpse
all you would mean
in the bitter end:
Thrill become thrall,
like alcohol.

How different then
things might have been.

Or different at all?
