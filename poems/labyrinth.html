Labyrinth

by John Enright

Gladly you walked the maze with me;
Though it was dark, though I could hear
The roaring of the Minotaur,
You stood beside me in my fear.

And when it grew too dark to see
What lay ahead, your voice was clear.
And so I stepped as one who slept,
And woke to find you standing near.
