Hyperion's Song of Fate

You range up there in the light,
On foot-friendly turf, godly Others!
Star-breezes sparkle and
Touch You as softly
As harp-women's fingers
Plucking the strings.

They breathe as free from fate
As a satified sleeping baby;
For Them the spirit
(Bud-like and modest,
Chastely protected)
Blossoms forever.
Blest are Their eyes
That gaze with eternal
Stillness and clarity.

But we, unlike Them, are denied
A restful place of our own.
All of humanity,
Faintingly, blindly,
Painfully stumbles
From minute to minute
A cataract tumbling
Down chasm to chasm,
Our destination unknown.

~

From the German of Friedrich Hölderlin.
