"Yellowstone" by Walter Kaufmann

Translated by Peter Saint-André

There where the water rushes
in spraying cascades among the depths
have I listened to the river
so many hours thoughtful and dreaming.

The water digs/cuts into/through the rock
where it winds/courses in/through grey ravines
from which the rock of a thousand towers
it finds its way upwards to the sun.

In dreams I've followed the stream
through woods and brambles always onward
and saw the world dissolved in the stream
beyond the goal and sense of all things.

~

From the German of Walter Kaufmann.
