Glossary of Maa Words

(Maa is the language of the Samburu, Maasai, and other related tribes, who live in the Northern Frontier District of Kenya.)

Baragoi -- a larger, more modern town south of Loiyangalani

Ilmugit -- "death of many cattle" a feast most often celebrating an age-set initiation or advancement among boys and men

Ilmugit Lelaingoni -- the ceremony of the bull, after which the older muran may marry and prepare to assume the responsibilities of the elders (10-12 years after initiation into muranhood)

Laibon -- a respected sage who is adviser, fortune teller, and herbal medicine man

Launoni -- a ritual leader of muran, an older boy from the clan

Lebarta -- traditional songs of initiate boys during the months of preparation for circumcision and Muranhood

Lngwesi -- a small, highly esteemed clan of Samburu into which Nalantei will be married

Loiyangalani -- a settlement of several tribes on the southern shore of Lake Turkana, named after a shrub that thrives near oases; it is the northern boundary of Samburuland

Longososi -- roots from this plant are prepared as medicines

Lorika -- a stool or headrest shaped from heavy tree roots

Ltorobo -- a distant, forest-dwelling clan of Samburu

Maralal -- a larger, more modern town south of Loiyangalani

Masigiu -- roots from this plant are prepared as medicines

Miraa -- a mild narcotic derived from an indigenous plant

Muran -- the warrior age-set, boys only, usually 12-26 years old

Ngai -- God

Ngaibartani -- a circumcised girl

Ndokuna -- a shaking fit or convulsion, occurring primarily among young men (muran) at times of great emotion or stress

Ng'iro -- the most sacred mountain of the Samburu

Nkang -- a circle of huts comprising an extended family or small settlement (kiswahili: manyatta)

Nkanyit -- the central tenet of the Samburu: respect and adherence to the tradition, culture, ceremony and rituals of life, especially as expressed in patriarchal rule

Nkarau -- a small vessel to hold liquids or fat, made from leather, woven palm, or wood

Nkoliontoi -- an uncircumsized girl

Rungu -- a short heavy stick with a bulbous end, used as a club (a "knobkerrie")

Samburu -- a tribe living in northern Kenya, of which Nalantei is a member

Shifta -- Somali bandits, often armed

Wamba -- a larger, more modern town south of Loiyangalani
